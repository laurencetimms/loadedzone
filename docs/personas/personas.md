# Zone 2 Loaded Walking — User Personas

## 1. Marcus, 44 — "The Optimiser"

**Who he is:** Software engineering manager at a mid-size fintech in Bristol. Two kids (8 and 11). Listened to every Peter Attia episode. Read *Outlive* twice. Has a Garmin Forerunner 265 and a Whoop strap. Tracks HRV, resting heart rate, sleep scores. Did CrossFit for five years, got injured, now lifting 3x/week with a coach.

**His zone 2 story:** Knows he *should* be doing zone 2 but finds the stationary bike soul-destroying. Tried jogging slowly but his heart rate spikes above zone 2 within minutes — he's too heavy and too unfit aerobically despite being strong. Heard Huberman mention weighted vest hiking and thought "wait, that's actually zone 2 I could do." Bought a 5.11 plate carrier on impulse. Did one walk. Loved it. But doesn't really know if he's doing it right or progressing.

**What he searches for:**
- "zone 2 heart rate calculator age weight"
- "weighted vest zone 2 training plan"
- "how heavy should my ruck be for zone 2"
- "garmin zone 2 settings rucking"
- "Pandolf equation calculator"

**What he wants from the site:**
- A calculator that tells him his exact zone 2 HR range using Karvonen, not just 220-age
- A reverse calculator: "I weigh 92kg, I want to be in zone 2 — what pack weight and pace?"
- Progression tracking: "Am I getting fitter? Is my HR at the same load dropping?"
- Garmin/Whoop setup guides specific to loaded walking
- The actual research, not dumbed-down listicles — he wants to see the Pandolf equation, the San Millán lactate curves, and understand *why*

**What he'd pay for:**
- Personalised 8-12 week zone 2 rucking programme (£3-5/month)
- Session logging with adaptation curves over time
- Export to Garmin Connect or TrainingPeaks

**His objections:**
- "Is this actually backed by research or is it bro science?"
- "I already have Garmin — why do I need another thing?"
- Won't tolerate a cluttered UI or gamification. Wants clean, data-forward, no badges.

**Tech setup:** Android phone (Pixel 8), Garmin Forerunner 265, laptop at home. Happy to use a web app on desktop and glance at his watch on the walk.

**Quote that captures him:** "I've got 4 hours a week for zone 2. I need to know I'm spending them right."

## 2. Rachel, 51 — "The Quiet Revolutionary"

**Who she is:** Deputy head at a secondary school in Oxfordshire. Perimenopausal. Two grown-up kids. Never been a "fitness person" — walked the dog, did yoga, occasional swim. GP told her bone density is declining and she should "do more weight-bearing exercise." A friend shared a Reel about weighted walking for menopause and something clicked. She doesn't want a gym. She doesn't want to run. She wants to walk — but smarter.

**Her zone 2 story:** Doesn't know what zone 2 is yet. Arrived at the concept through the bone density / perimenopause / weighted walking pipeline on Instagram. Bought a cheap weighted vest from Amazon (8kg). Wears it on her morning dog walk. Feels good but has no idea if she's in the "right" zone, the right weight, or whether she's actually getting health benefits beyond "it feels harder."

**What she searches for:**
- "weighted walking benefits menopause"
- "how heavy should weighted vest be for a woman"
- "heart rate zones explained simply"
- "walking for bone density how much weight"
- "zone 2 training for beginners over 50"

**What she wants from the site:**
- A clear, jargon-free explanation of what zone 2 is and why it matters for her specifically (bone density, metabolic health, longevity)
- A simple calculator: "Here's your zone 2 range. Here's how heavy to start. Here's how long to walk."
- A printable session card she can glance at on her walk (she leaves her phone at home with the dog lead)
- Reassurance that she's doing enough — or guidance to do a bit more
- Research translated into plain language: "A study found X — for someone your age, that means Y"

**What she'd pay for:**
- A structured 8-week "start loaded walking" programme
- A seasonal plan that adapts to weather/daylight (she walks at 6:30am before school)
- Possibly an app that her Apple Watch can run standalone (no phone needed)

**Her objections:**
- "Is this going to be full of military stuff? That's not me."
- "I don't understand all these heart rate formulas."
- Intimidated by tech-heavy fitness content. Needs warmth, clarity, and evidence — not data dashboards.
- Won't tolerate a condescending "for women!" pink aesthetic either.

**Tech setup:** iPhone 14, Apple Watch SE (2nd gen), uses it mainly for steps and occasionally checking heart rate. Doesn't know how to set up heart rate zones. Comfortable with websites on her phone but hasn't downloaded a fitness app since Couch to 5K years ago.

**Quote that captures her:** "I just want to know I'm actually doing myself some good on these walks."

## 3. Dan, 37 — "The HIIT Refugee"

**Who he is:** Account director at a creative agency in Manchester. Single, rents a flat in Ancoats. Was doing F45 and Barry's Bootcamp 4-5x/week for three years. Got burnt out. Cortisol through the roof, sleep terrible, started getting niggling injuries. A mate who's a PT told him he was "all zone 4, no base." Started reading about zone 2 and polarised training. Felt like someone had explained what was wrong with his entire approach.

**His zone 2 story:** Tried slow jogging for zone 2 but felt ridiculous shuffling along at 10min/km pace. Ego problem. Then discovered that adding a weighted pack meant he could walk at a normal pace and actually be in zone 2. Game changer. Now does three 45-min loaded walks a week — one on his lunch break, two before work. Feels dramatically better. Sleeps better. Wants to get more systematic about it.

**What he searches for:**
- "zone 2 training without running"
- "polarised training plan walking"
- "rucking vs running calories heart rate"
- "how to combine zone 2 walking with strength training"
- "zone 2 walking weight vest urban"

**What he wants from the site:**
- A weekly planner that integrates zone 2 loaded walks with his 2-3 gym sessions
- Calorie/energy expenditure comparison: "Here's what 45 mins of loaded walking gives you vs. the same time running or cycling"
- Heart rate drift tracking — he's noticed his HR is lower at the same load/pace after 6 weeks and wants to see that visualised
- Urban route considerations: "How do traffic lights and stop-starts affect zone 2 training?"
- Social proof that this is a legit training approach, not "just walking"

**What he'd pay for:**
- An integrated programme that blends zone 2 loaded walking with strength training in a coherent periodisation
- The ability to log sessions quickly (30 seconds, not 5 minutes of data entry)
- A "what's my equivalent" converter — if I rucked 5km with 15kg, what's that equivalent to in terms of a run?

**His objections:**
- "Walking with a backpack — is anyone actually going to take this seriously?"
- Needs the framing to feel progressive and evidence-based, not like a step backwards from "real training"
- Wants something that looks good on his phone — design-conscious, not a utility-first fitness app from 2015

**Tech setup:** iPhone 15 Pro, Apple Watch Ultra 2, AirPods. Heavy Strava user. Would want Strava integration or at minimum the ability to cross-reference.

**Quote that captures him:** "I wasted three years going as hard as possible. Turns out the smart move was slowing down and adding weight."

## 4. Janet, 63 — "The Longevity Investor"

**Who she is:** Retired NHS physiotherapist in the Cotswolds. Husband still works part-time. Grandchildren she wants to keep up with for the next 20 years. Stays active — walks 5 miles most days, does Pilates twice a week, swims on Fridays. Diagnosed with osteopenia at her last DEXA scan. Her daughter sent her the Peter Attia "Centenarian Decathlon" concept and it hit her like a truck — she'd been assuming "staying active" was enough, but now she's thinking much more specifically about what she needs to be able to do at 80.

**Her zone 2 story:** Already inadvertently doing zone 2 on her long walks — her heart rate naturally sits at 60-65% of max during brisk walking. But she's read that adding load could help with bone density *and* keep her in zone 2 (her brisk walk sometimes dips into zone 1 because she's quite aerobically fit). Bought a light rucksack and started with 4kg. Wants to know: is this enough? Should it be more? How does this interact with her Pilates and swimming?

**What she searches for:**
- "weighted walking osteopenia bone density evidence"
- "zone 2 heart rate for 63 year old woman"
- "how much weight to carry walking for bone health"
- "Peter Attia centenarian decathlon exercises"
- "safe rucking over 60"

**What she wants from the site:**
- Research specifically about load-bearing exercise and bone density, translated clearly with citations she can show her GP
- A conservative, safety-conscious progression plan — she's a physiotherapist, she knows about joint loading and injury risk
- Clarity on how loaded walking interacts with her existing routine (Pilates, swimming, regular walking)
- The ability to calculate zone 2 using her actual known resting HR (she takes it every morning) rather than crude age-based formulas
- Printable session plans — she doesn't want to carry her phone on walks

**What she'd pay for:**
- A year-long periodised plan for someone her age with osteopenia
- Detailed guides on form, posture, and injury prevention during loaded walking
- Access to a reference library of the actual research papers with plain-language summaries

**Her objections:**
- "Most fitness content is written by 28-year-olds for 28-year-olds."
- Needs to feel that the site takes safety seriously — not just "more weight = better"
- Would want to see credentials or citations, not influencer energy
- Doesn't want an app — wants a website she can read on her iPad and print from

**Tech setup:** iPhone 12, Apple Watch Series 7 (gift from daughter), iPad Air at home. Reasonably tech-comfortable but doesn't want to learn new systems. Prints things out. Bookmarks pages in Safari.

**Quote that captures her:** "I want to be picking up my grandchildren when I'm 80. Tell me exactly what I need to do."

- "minimum zone 2 session length research"

**Quote that captures him:** "If I can turn my commute into legitimate zone 2 training, that's an extra hour a day I didn't have."

## Persona Usage Notes

**When evaluating a feature idea, ask:**
1. Which personas does this serve?
2. Does any persona actively *not* want this? (If so, can it be optional/hidden?)
3. Would Marcus share it? Would Rachel understand it? Would Janet trust it? Would Kai cite it?
4. Does it respect the "no phone on walk" users (Rachel, Janet) as much as the data-trackers (Marcus, Dan, Kai)?

**Demographic coverage:**
- Age range: 29-63
- Gender: 3M / 2F (reflects current rucking skew but zone 2 audience is much closer to 50/50)
- Tech sophistication: ranges from Kai (will read your API docs) to Janet (will print a PDF)
- Fitness level: ranges from Rachel (dog walker) to Marcus (ex-CrossFit lifter)
- Entry point: ranges from "zone 2" (Marcus, Dan, Kai) to "bone density" (Rachel, Janet)
- Willingness to pay: ranges from Kai (free tier evangelist) to Marcus (instant subscriber if the tool is good)

**Key insight across all personas:**
Every single one of them wants the same core thing — *"What does the research mean for ME, specifically?"* — but they need it delivered at different levels of complexity, different tones, and through different interfaces.
